About the School for the Blind

The School for the Blind was established on June 24, 1831, located in New York. This school aims to provide accurate, reliable, and advanced educational services for visually impaired students. Our school strives to be a leader in providing high-quality and reliable education, contributing to the improvement of public health and well-being of our students. With an unwavering commitment to accuracy, innovation, and professional ethics, we will use the most advanced technologies and best practices to ensure accurate and rapid education. We aim to create an environment where our dedicated staff can develop professionally and provide exceptional service to our students and partners.

Programs

Readiness

Children 3-5 have been referred to the program through their local Committee on Preschool Special Education, and display a developmental delay in one or more of the following areas: cognitive, speech and language, fine or gross motor skills, sensory integration, adaptive or social-emotional development.

The schermerhorn

The Schermerhorn Program has a 190-year history of serving students with blindness and visual impairments. It offers diverse educational services to meet the needs of children who are legally blind, from the ages of 5 to 21. Students participate in individually designed academic and modified academic programs that emphasize independence.
